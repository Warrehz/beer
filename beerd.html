Ale

Are you up for a more complex, intense flavor ? Check out an ale! Ale production can be traced back more than 5000 years, making it by far the oldest type of beer. Some styles in this beer type include pale ale, red ale, blonde ale, brown ale, and wheat beer.

Examples - Newcastle, Killian's Red, Sierra Nevada Pale Ale

Made in many varieties, ales are typically brewed with top fermenting yeast and are fuller-bodied with a hoppy finish.

Lager

Are you looking for an easy-drinking crowd-pleaser? If so, a lager may be the way to go. Though considerably younger than ales, lagers are vastly more popular than their counterpart. Lagers are distinguished by their significantly cleaner finish. Popular varieties include pilsners, bocks, and dunkels.

Examples - Budweiser, Shiner Bock, Heineken

The world's most popular beer type, lagers are crisp, bottom-fermented brews with a smooth finish.
